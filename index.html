Hart-Devlin Debate: A Comparison Table

Basis of Comparison H.L.A. Hart's View Lord Devlin's View
Role of the state The state has no legitimate authority to interfere in the private lives of its citizens The state has a duty to protect the moral fabric of society
Relationship between morality and law Morality and the law are two separate spheres of human activity Morality and the law are intimately connected
Principles of the law The law should be based on principles of individual liberty and personal autonomy The law should reflect the moral values of society
The scope of the law The law should only be concerned with actions that directly harm others The law should be concerned with actions that are perceived to harm society as a whole
Individual freedom The law should respect individual freedom and autonomy The law should limit individual freedom to protect the collective good
Social order The law should not be used to impose moral values on society The law should reflect the moral values of society in order to maintain social order and cohesion
Relationship between law and morality Law is a social institution that is designed to regulate human behavior Law and morality are intertwined and law is used to promote and enforce moral values
Autonomy The individual should have the right to live their life as they see fit, as long as they are not causing harm to others Individual autonomy should be limited to ensure that it does not conflict with the moral values of society
The harm principle The law should only restrict individual freedom if it can be shown that the individual's behavior is causing harm to others The harm principle is not sufficient, and the law should be used ... to restrict individual freedom in order to protect society as a whole, even if no direct harm is being caused.
Criminal law Criminal law should only be used to prohibit actions that directly harm others or infringe on their rights Criminal law should be used to enforce the moral values of society, even if no direct harm is being caused
Emphasis Emphasized individual liberty and autonomy Emphasised on collective good and the maintenance of social order.
Thesis Separability Thesis:Hart saw morality and the law as separate spheres. Overlap Thesis: Devlin believed that the law should reflect the moral values of society.
Summary Hart believed that the law should be used to regulate human behavior only insofar as it caused harm to others, and that individuals should be free to live their lives as they see fit, without excessive interference from the state. Devlin, on the other hand, believed that the law should be used to promote and enforce the most important moral values held by society, even if this requires some degree of interference in the lives of individuals.
